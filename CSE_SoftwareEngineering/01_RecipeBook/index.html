Beef Stroganoff

Ingredients

  • 1 Onion
  • 1 lb Beef
  • 1 can Cream of Mushroom Soup
  • Sour Cream
  • Thickening Agent: Flour, Corn Starch, etc.
  • Spices: Salt, Pepper, Paprika

Cooking Steps

  1. Saute onions in pan until partially translucent, then set aside from pan.
  2. Brown the beef in the pan
  3. Add the onions back to the pan
  4. Add soup, spices and thickening agent
  5. Mix thoroughly, adding water to reduce viscosity, or adding more thickening agent to increase viscosity.
  6. Simmer mixture for 10 minutes
  7. Add sour cream to appropriate levels
  8. Mix thoroughly and let simmer for 5 minutes

Creme Brulee

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Heavy Cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon Vanilla Extract
  • 8 Egg Yolks
  • 1/2 cup Sugar

Cooking Steps

  1. Mix sugar and egg yolks thoroughly
  2. Heat heavy cream until almost simmering
  3. Gently add heated cream to yolk mixture, one tablespoon at a time, stirring with vigor
  4. After one-quarter to one-eighth of the cream has been added, pour the rest in and mix well
  5. Strain the mixture using a fine mesh sieve. Repeat this step one or two additional times for a better final product
  6. Add the vanilla extract and mix thoroughly
  7. Portion the mixture into 6-8 ramekins
  8. Place the ramekins into a deep baking pan, fill pan with boiling water up to half the height of your ramekins
  9. Place pan in (preheated) oven set to 250 degrees fahrenheit for 60 minutes
  10. Set ramekins at room temperature until cooled
  11. Wrap ramekins with plastic and place in fridge to cool and preserve.

Serving Instruction

  1. One hour before serving, remove plastic wrap and gently tap-dry the top of the custards with a paper towel.
  2. Add a layer of sugar turbinado (sugar in the raw) to the top of each dessert
  3. Caramelize the sugar layer with a blowtorch or similar tool, will take roughly 1 minute for each dessert.
  4. Refrigerate desserts for remaining time (30-45 minutes) before serving.
  5. For the best dining experience, serve desserts within an hour of torching, as waiting longer will allow the sugar to dissolve into the custard below.
